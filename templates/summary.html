Measuring all the noises of LLM evals

Results Paper Code

Versions: main, train_curve, Open models with vLLM: T=0.7, T=1, high K T=0.7,

Summary of the results with more summary stats are available on mouse over and details linked.
  • size: number of questions in the eval
  • models: the number of models used on this eval
  • SE(A): the standard error of a single score on each question (SE_x for data noise) in percent. A difference of 1.96*sqrt(2)*SE(A) is the conventional standard for p=0.05 significance
  • SE(A-B): the total paired standard error of a single score on each question (SE_x for data noise) in percent. A difference of 1.96*SE(A-B) is the conventional standard for p=0.05 significance
  • corr(A,B): correlation of expected scores in percent
  • no_solve: percent questions not solved by any models
  • tau-: percent questions negatively correlated with the overall model quality as measured by Kendall's tau
  • details: aggregating by the models; by examples; the prediction heatmap; and raw data tables.
